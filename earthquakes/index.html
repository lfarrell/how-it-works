Introduction

Movement of Earthquake Energy

An earthquake first sends out a cosine wave. Thie is a slow moving wave with a low periodcity. Periodcity is the crest from one wave to the next crest.

These are followed by sine waves, with much higher periodcity. This type of wave causes the destruction commonly associated with earthquakes and can last from several seconds for small quakes to five minutes or beyond for the largest quakes.
